# Practice Yes And SEO Keyword Strategy

## Goal

This document defines the SEO keyword strategy for Practice Yes And.

The site homepage is both:

- the main SEO landing page
- the main product experience page

That means the homepage should stay tightly focused on the highest-intent core keywords, while broader scenario keywords should be captured through blog content that routes users back to the homepage tool.

## Product Positioning

Practice Yes And helps users practice the "Yes, And" mindset through conversation practice.

Core product capabilities:

- generate better responses in tricky situations
- practice conversation scenarios
- improve communication through guided roleplay
- apply Yes, And thinking in real life
- cover scenarios across work, relationships, parenting, and social life

## SEO Strategy

### Homepage Role

The homepage should focus on the two core keyword themes:

- `yes and practice`
- `conversation practice`

These should anchor the homepage title, H1, description, hero copy, FAQ, and supporting section headings.

The homepage should not try to rank equally for every scenario-specific keyword. If too many themes are pushed into the homepage, topic relevance becomes diluted.

### Blog Role

The blog should capture broader search demand through:

- scenario keywords
- educational keywords
- question-based keywords

Each article should link back to the homepage product experience with clear calls to action.

## Keywords To Avoid As Primary Targets

These can be mentioned where relevant, but they should not be the main SEO focus of the homepage.

- `yes and`
- `yes and game`
- `improv game`
- `team building game`

Reason:

- these terms are often too broad
- they can attract low-intent traffic
- they can skew search relevance toward games, activities, workshops, or entertainment instead of communication practice

## Homepage Execution Guidance

The homepage should be optimized around one clear idea:

Practice the Yes, And mindset through guided conversation practice.

Recommended direction:

- H1 should combine `Yes And Practice` and `Conversation Practice`
- hero copy should mention improving responses, difficult conversations, and real-life scenarios
- supporting sections should reinforce communication practice, roleplay, and active listening
- FAQ should answer what Yes, And means, how it helps communication, and who it is for

## Blog Execution Guidance

Blog content should:

- target one scenario or question per page
- match a specific search intent
- teach something useful
- naturally introduce the product as a way to practice
- link users back to the homepage tool

Recommended post types:

- scenario roundups
- how-to articles
- example-driven posts
- mindset explanation posts
